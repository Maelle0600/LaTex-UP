\chapter{Aim of this Thesis}
\label{chap:aim}
The aim of this thesis was to investigate mitochondrial metabolism in distinct cell types of the developing neocortex at E14.5 using genome-scale metabolic modeling. While transcriptomic data provide valuable information for the reconstruction of context-specific metabolic models, additional biochemical constraints are often required to better approximate cellular physiology. In particular, conventional FBA relies on the definition of a biologically meaningful objective function, which is difficult to justify in developing and transitional cell states where mitochondria fulfill diverse functions beyond ATP production. To address these limitations, cell type-specific oxygen uptake constraints were defined based on literature evidence and the E-Flux integration framework together with a gene expression-guided model extraction approach which is inspired by \textit{Shlomi et al.}
This strategy enabled the reconstruction of cell type-specific mitochondrial submodels and the identification of core metabolic reactions associated with each cell type.
